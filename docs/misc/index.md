# Miscellaneous

The reference material that underpins the condition pages rather than a condition in its own right, biomechanics, instrumentation, and the general complications of spinal surgery that don't belong to one specific pathology.

Planned pages:

- Spinal biomechanics and instability, the concepts referenced across the Trauma and Deformity sections rather than repeated on each page
- Spinal instrumentation, screws, rods, cages, and interbody devices, what they are before diving into when to use them
- Complications of spinal surgery in general (surgical site infection, neurological deterioration, dural tear), as distinct from the complications specific to one operation covered on that operation's own page
- Post-operative rehabilitation principles

Like Radiology, this section is reference material rather than a single evidence-driven topic, so it's a lower priority for literature searching and a higher priority for clear original diagrams.
