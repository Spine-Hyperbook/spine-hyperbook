## Evidence base

| Study | Key finding |
|---|---|
| Peul WC et al., Surgery versus prolonged conservative treatment for sciatica, *NEJM* 2007 | Early surgery gave faster relief of leg pain than prolonged conservative care, but outcomes converged by one year. |
| Weinstein JN et al., SPORT trial, *JAMA* 2006 | As-treated analysis favoured surgery, but very high crossover between arms limited the intention-to-treat result. |
| Liu C et al., Surgical versus non-surgical treatment for sciatica: systematic review and meta-analysis of RCTs, *BMJ* 2023 | Evidence favouring surgery rated low-certainty overall; benefit over conservative care is short-to-medium term, barely detectable at one year. |
