# Lumbar Spinal Stenosis & Degenerative Spondylolisthesis

!!! abstract "Curriculum mapping"
    **T&O curriculum:** The aging spine and degenerative disease, Basic Science > Pathology (p.52, level 2/4/4). Compressive conditions (radicular/stenotic/myelopathic), Treatments > Operative (p.53, level 1/4/4).

    **Skills:** Decompression lumbar spine without fusion (p.67, level 0/2/4); decompression with fusion +/- fixation (p.67, level 0/1/4).

## Introduction

Degenerative narrowing of the spinal canal, lateral recess, or neural foramen, most commonly from facet joint hypertrophy and ligamentum flavum thickening, causing neurogenic claudication and/or radiculopathy.

## Clinical presentation

- Neurogenic claudication: leg pain/heaviness worse on walking and standing, relieved by flexion (leaning on a trolley, sitting)
- Distinguish from vascular claudication: neurogenic pain typically eases with flexion regardless of continued walking; vascular claudication eases only with rest
- Radiological severity correlates poorly with symptom severity, treat the patient, not the scan

## Investigation

MRI lumbar spine is first line. Note the grade of central/lateral recess/foraminal stenosis but weigh this against the clinical picture.

## Management

- Conservative: physiotherapy, weight management, analgesia, epidural steroid injection for symptom control
- Surgical: decompression (laminectomy, unilateral laminotomy for bilateral decompression, or less invasive approaches)

!!! question "Controversy: does degenerative spondylolisthesis need fusion alongside decompression?"
    This is the best-evidenced controversy in the entire spine syllabus, three major RCTs now broadly converge.

    | Study | Design | Finding |
    |---|---|---|
    | Försth et al. (Swedish Spinal Stenosis Study), *NEJM* 2016 | 247 patients, decompression alone vs decompression + fusion | No significant difference in ODI at 2 or 5 years. Fusion group had longer hospital stay, more bleeding, higher cost. |
    | Ghogawala et al., *NEJM* 2016 | 66 patients, grade I spondylolisthesis only | Some outcomes favoured fusion, diverged from Försth, fuelled ongoing debate about entry criteria. |
    | NORDSTEN-DS trial, *NEJM* 2021 | Noninferiority design | Decompression alone was noninferior to decompression with instrumented fusion. |
    | *Bone Joint J* 2022, SSSS 2-year MRI follow-up | Radiological outcomes | Decompression alone did not increase restenosis or proximal adjacent-level stenosis vs fusion. |

    **Working position:** routine fusion adds cost, blood loss and hospital stay without a consistent functional benefit for straightforward degenerative spondylolisthesis with stenosis. Reserve fusion for frank instability, deformity, or recurrent stenosis.
