# Radiology

Not yet built. BOFAS's Hyperbook runs this as a numbered series of short imaging-focused pages (normal anatomy, classic pathological appearances, measurement techniques) rather than folding imaging into every condition page.

Planned scope:

1. Normal radiographic anatomy, cervical, thoracic, lumbar
2. Measurement techniques referenced elsewhere on the site (Cobb angle, Pavlov ratio, SINS score, Bilsky grading), collected here as a single reference point rather than re-explained on every page that uses them
3. MRI basics for trainees who haven't done a radiology attachment, sequence types, what "T2 hyperintensity" actually means
4. Classic appearances by pathology (OPLL on CT, discitis on MRI, metastatic disease patterns)

This section is lower priority for literature searching than the condition pages, it's mostly stable anatomical and technique knowledge, and higher priority for good original annotated images, which is a different kind of authoring task.
